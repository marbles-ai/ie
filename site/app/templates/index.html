About marbles.ai

Marbles.ai is a small group of passionate engineers working on the hard Natural Language Processing (NLP) problems plaguing our society. After reviewing existing technologies, we came to realize that there isn't a robust, existing solution to the most fundamental of questions: What does this text, written by a human, mean? This may seem like a fairly straight-forward problem, but it's also very difficult. This is clearly demonstrated by today's 'Intelligent Personal Assistants' like Siri and Cortana. These cutting-edge products that run on massive computing clusters can't understand simple queries.

Looking beyond that, Fake News has become a big problem over the past two years. The openness of the Internet has allowed trolls to hijack human minds by presenting 'fake news' in a official package. This is particularly sinister, because it is often difficult to differentiate these sources from official sources on the egalitarian Internet.

We have solved these problems by extending cutting-edge NLP research with Machine Learning techniques and representing knowledge extracted from natural language text in a clear abstraction we call Marbles. A Marble is a piece of knowledge gained from text, and we represent complex ideas and conversations with large networks of Marbles connected by Smart Edges.

So what does our competition do? Event Registry also uses an NLP pipeline. They use tokenization and Part of Speech (POS) tagging along with entity detection and disambiguation to extract names, dates and events. This allows them to rapidly coellece data from ?hundreds? of sources and look for like terms. This is a useful way of detecting news events and potentially identifying outliers.

Our approach goes much further. We represent the sentences in the raw data in a semantic and discourse-aware representation that alows us to also understand the meaning of the data. We then represent the data with our marbles network representation that allows us to compute relationships between networks looking for:

  • the primary source of the data
  • biases in the data
