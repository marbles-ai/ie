Fake News!

Fake news is having a significant impact on our society! It not only allows bad actors to influence other countries' elections, but it is also making it difficult for citizens to understand what is going on around them and make their politicians accountable for their actions.

We solve the problem of identifying Fake News by using a combination of state of the art Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning techniques to analyze information as it comes in. We gather information using our large selection of RSS feeds from websites, Twitter, Facebook, etc. This information is transformed from its raw form into a machine-readable semantic discourse representation that we call Marbles. These Marbles represent the semantics and knowledge represented in the information gathered.

We then use both supervised and unsupervised Machine Learning techniques to determine the source of information, which we call "Golden Marbles", and both similarities and differences between different sources' Marbles. This allows us to find outlier Marbles that deviate from Golden Marbles. Our technique allows for a much deeper understanding of the information, and it also allows us to evaluate the factualness of information without considering the source. This is particularly important, because of the growing disagreement between members of differing political parties with relation to the factualness of different information sources.
